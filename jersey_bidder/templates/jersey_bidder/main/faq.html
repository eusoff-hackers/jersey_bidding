FAQ


Can't find the answer you are looking for? We have shared some of our most frequently asked questions to help you out!

What is the overall process?

Jersey bidding 19/20 will take place over the course of 4 days with 4 different waves of bidding and each gender will have 99 numbers to choose from (1-99)

Seniors who have taken part in three or more IHG seasons will bid first in the first wave followed by seniors with two IHG seasons, followed by seniors with one IHG season and lastly freshmen or seniors with no IHG participation will bid in the final wave.

Can we share number with other Eusoffians?

For Eusoffians who have participated in at least 2 IHG seasons, sharing is not allowed by default unless he or she agrees to share (this preference can be edited in the 'bid' page).

For Euosffian who have participated in 1 IHG or less (generally year 1 and year 2), sharing of number is possible unless they have clashing sports.

In order for two Eusoffians to share a number, they must not have any sport in common.

What do we have to do?

We will notify you when it is your turn to bid via Channel EH. Each wave will start at 6 AM and ends at 9 PM on the same day.

When it is your turn, you can first use the 'Indicate Preference' page to see potential bidders for a number. Once you have made your decision, go to the 'Bid' page to put down your top 5 choices.

After 9 PM, the system will not accept anymore bidding submissions. Numbers will be allocated to the bidders in this wave and bidders can use 'Check Result' to see their number.

What to do if I miss my bidding window?

Contact Brenda or Jiaying or any SMC Logistics member to notify them. SMC Logistics will then allocate you a number randomly.

How does 'indicate preference' work?

In this page, you can see which number is really popular and their potential bidders. You can also use this page to deconflict with fellow Eusoffians if you happen to be wanting the same number. Indicating your preference in this page also helps other Eusoffians to make more informed decisions. .

Who gets to bid first and why?

Priority to bid is determined by how many times an Euosffian have participated in IHG. For example, a year 4 senior who has taken part in 3 IHGs will have a higher priority than a year 2 Eusoffian who has only taken part in 1.

Note that year of study does not imply pirority - a year 2 Eusoffian who has taken part in 1 IHG has a higher priority than a year 3 senior who has never taken part in IHG before. If the number of times of participation is the same between two Euosffians, priority is determined by Bidding Points (see below).

How are our bidding points determined?

Bidding Point is the sum of the following:
  • number of sports an Eusoffian is in (after first cut), each sport constitute as 1 point.
  • leadership position in sports, each leadership role (captain only) constitute as 1 point.
  • if an Eusoffian is representing NUS in any sports, each IVP/Sunig sport constitute as 1 point.

For example, if Alice is in the NUS floorball team, is the captain of Eusoff Floorball and is in both Eusoff floorball and Football
then Alice will have: 1 (NUS floorball) + 1 (Captain) + 2 (Eusoff Floorball and Football) = 4 points

What if two Eusoffians have the exact same priority and bidding points?

The order will be randomly sorted.

Is Bidding Compulsory?

All Eusoffians are entitled to bid for a jersey number :)

However, bidding is mandatory for atheletes from the following sports: Frisbee, Softball, Takraw, Netball, Floorball, Soccer, Handball, Volleyball
